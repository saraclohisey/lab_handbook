**Preface.**

This document communicates my expectations for people working in this research group. In the spirit of good communication, it reflects an ongoing discussion. Nothing is unchangeable, but I ask you to respect the process and abide by the expectations written here unless we agree to a change.

**What I expect of myself.**

I aim to be the best advisor I can be to each of my trainees. My goals include:
•	Providing each person with first-rate training in experimental design and precise, scientific thinking to the best of my ability.
•	Ensuring every trainee is continually provided with learning opportunities while in my lab.
•	Providing each trainee with guidance, recommendation letters, and networking opportunities based on their personal career goals (not mine).
•	Treating everyone fairly and making sure they are included in the culture of the lab.
•	Making sure everyone gets credit for the work they have done and promoting those who are on the job market in my public talks.
•	Ensuring that manuscripts are submitted and published in a timely manner. A solid record of publication is the key to success.
•	Listening and improving based on your suggestions. Let me know if you have other expectations of me as a mentor. I am always happy to receive constructive feedback in person. Alternatively, here is a link to offer anonymous feedback.

**What I expect of you.**

•	Regular, punctual attendance at lab meetings, with active participation in discussions
•	Performance of experiments in accordance with all safety guidelines and regulations surrounding animal research and controlled substances
•	Personal responsibility for your academic progress and/or career goals
•	Responsibility for meeting the deadlines we agree on together
•	Proactive intellectual contributions to your project
•	Open sharing of protocols, code, and raw data (within the lab pre-publication, outside the lab post-publication), along with good record-keeping and project management, such that others can repeat, verify, and extend your findings
•	Good lab citizenship - helping to maintain a clean, organized, and friendly environment
•	“Mentoring up” - communication of your personal needs to me so that I can help you
•	If you are a graduate student or postdoc:
o	Completion of at least one first-author paper on your project
o	At least one presentation of your work at a national/international conference
